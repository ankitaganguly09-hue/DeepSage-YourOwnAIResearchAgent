**Research Report: Durga Puja in Kolkata 2026**

**Introduction**

Durga Puja is one of the most significant cultural events in West Bengal, celebrated with immense enthusiasm and fervor in Kolkata. This annual Hindu festival honors the goddess Durga and spans five days, typically falling in the months of September or October. This report aims to provide a detailed overview of Durga Puja in Kolkata for the year 2026, including key dates, celebrations, locations, cultural significance, and community engagement.

**Key Findings**

1. **Event Overview and Dates for 2026**
   - Durga Puja in Kolkata is a major annual festival celebrated with great enthusiasm. The festival is expected to be celebrated from 17th to 21st October 2026, with the main celebrations occurring from the 6th to the 10th day of the Hindu calendar month of Ashwin (Mark, 2026).

2. **Celebrations and Locations**
   - The festival is celebrated across various locations in Kolkata, including North Kolkata and South Kolkata. These areas are renowned for their elaborate pandals (temporary structures) and vibrant decorations (Best, 2026).
   - The Kolkata Durga Puja Parikrama is a notable event that includes a guided tour of the most famous pandals. This event is scheduled for specific days such as Saptami (18th October 2026) and Nabami (20th October 2026) (Kolkata, 2026).

3. **Cultural Significance**
   - The UNESCO tag to Kolkata's Durga Puja has significantly boosted tourism in the region, making it a major attraction for both domestic and international visitors. This recognition highlights the cultural and historical importance of the festival (Durga, 2026).

4. **Community Engagement**
   - Durga Puja is a time for community gatherings, cultural performances, and traditional feasts. It is a period when people come together to celebrate and enjoy various cultural activities, fostering a sense of unity and shared heritage.

**Conclusion**

Durga Puja in Kolkata is a vibrant and culturally rich festival that attracts a significant number of visitors each year. The festival's dates for 2026 are confirmed to be from 17th to 21st October, with elaborate celebrations and pandals across the city. The UNESCO recognition of Kolkata's Durga Puja underscores its cultural significance and global appeal. The festival serves as a platform for community engagement, cultural performances, and traditional feasts, making it a must-visit event for both locals and tourists.
